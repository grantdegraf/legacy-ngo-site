About the Project

Women Without Fear is a historical initiative dedicated to recovering and preserving the documented stories of thirty Jewish women who actively resisted the Nazi regime between 1933 and 1945. Through a comprehensive book and a 30-episode cinematic series, the project returns their names, their choices, and their courage to living memory.

Why This Matters Now

As the last generation of direct witnesses passes, the obligation to document and transmit these accounts becomes more urgent. Women Without Fear fills a documented gap in Holocaust historiography: the active, often organized resistance by Jewish women whose stories have been systematically overlooked in mainstream historical accounts.

Their courage was real. Their names deserve to be known.

The Book

The Women Without Fear volume presents thirty thoroughly researched biographies, each grounded in archival evidence, survivor testimonies, and scholarly sources. The book is structured to serve both general readers and academic audiences, with full citations and a comprehensive appendix.

Each chapter tells one life in full — the circumstances, the resistance, the cost, and the legacy.

The Series

Thirty episodes. Thirty women. Each cinematic episode is a self-contained production dedicated to one subject, drawing on the historical record to reconstruct moments of extraordinary courage with dignity and precision. The series is designed for broad distribution, including broadcast, streaming, and educational licensing.

Educational Partnerships

Women Without Fear is actively seeking formal partnerships with Holocaust education organizations, museums, cultural institutions, and foundations. The project offers curriculum-aligned materials, screening licenses, and collaborative research frameworks.

Grounded in the Record

Every subject in Women Without Fear is documented. The project draws on archives, published scholarship, and primary testimony. No claims are invented. No stories are composite. The project is committed to the highest standards of historical accuracy and to treating its subjects with the respect their lives demand.
