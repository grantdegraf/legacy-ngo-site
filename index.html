What We Stand For

Legacy Project Objectives

  • ◈
    Build narrative defenses against antisemitism. Antisemitism does not retreat on its own — it requires structured, documented counter-narrative. We produce the content and tools that communities need to push back with precision and conviction.
  • ◈
    Produce documentaries and films that move audiences to understanding. Cinema has always been where history becomes visceral. We make films that are rigorously sourced and emotionally honest — built for broadcast, streaming, and educational distribution.
  • ◈
    Translate Holocaust history into active education. Knowledge without application is inert. Our education initiatives are designed to close the distance between historical record and lived responsibility — in classrooms, institutions, and communities.
  • ◈
    Equip communities with content and tools they can use. From curriculum-aligned materials to screening licenses and research frameworks, we build resources designed for institutional adoption and long-term use.
  • ◈
    Honor courage through real, documented stories. Every subject in our work is verified. No composites. No invention. The record exists — and the people in it deserve to have their stories told with the same precision and care they brought to their own lives.
  • ◈
    Create a legacy that outlasts the moment. Donors who support this work are not funding a transaction — they are building something that will be taught, screened, and cited long after the donation is made. That is the architecture of lasting impact.

Featured Project

Women Without Fear
Book & Documentary Project

Women Without Fear is a book and cinematic documentary series documenting thirty Jewish women who actively resisted the Nazi regime between 1933 and 1945. Each subject is drawn from the verified historical record — no composites, no invention. These are real people who made real decisions under conditions designed to eliminate them, and whose strategies, networks, and courage have been systematically underrepresented in mainstream historical accounts.

The book presents thirty thoroughly researched biographies, each grounded in archival evidence, survivor testimonies, and published scholarship. The documentary series adapts each life into a self-contained cinematic episode, designed for broadcast, streaming, and educational licensing globally. The series is produced to the standard of institutional documentary — rigorous sourcing, precise reconstruction, dignity as a non-negotiable.

This work matters now because antisemitism is accelerating, the last generation of direct witnesses is passing, and the documented record of Jewish women’s resistance is still incompletely known. Women Without Fear closes that gap — for educators, for cultural institutions, for donors who want their support to do something durable.
